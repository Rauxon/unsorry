Gate A's `gate-a-prepare` now publishes its freshly-built library oleans to the ADR-045 fallback cache on **PRs**, not just `main` (ADR-094 / SPEC-094-A, #5751). After the audit (ADR-091) and replay (ADR-063) were sharded across runners, a shard whose per-runner Namespace `.lake` volume missed had no exact-commit handoff from `prepare` on a PR (the publish was main-only) and could **cold-rebuild the whole library (~21–45 min)** — the 1077 s shard tail seen in the D1a production measurement. Publishing on PRs gives every shard of the run an exact-`<sha>` restore hit, so its `lake build UnsorryLibrary --wfail` is an incremental no-op instead of a cold build — removing the tail and cutting runner-minutes per PR (the cost that bounds steady-state throughput). The step stays `continue-on-error` and non-gating: a failed or LRU-evicted save degrades to the previous prefix-restore behaviour, so the change is a strict superset. Soundness is unchanged — `leanchecker`/`axiom_audit` re-check every restored olean and Lake rebuilds any whose source changed; no contributor artifact is trusted, and no artifact-download is introduced.
