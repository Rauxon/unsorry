Added a **declaration-scoped export** mode to the D2 pilot (`--scope-decls`, ADR-093, #5684): instead of exporting a whole library module (whose `import Mathlib` pulls the full ~5.9 GB mathlib closure), it exports only the module's own theorem(s) and their transitive *declaration* closure (`lean4export Module -- <theorem>`). This is the experiment for Q4 (the export-size blocker): a single proof's declaration closure may be far smaller than its module's import closure. Running scoped also yields a fairer Q3 nanoda-speed number on the smaller export. Observe-only; gates nothing.
