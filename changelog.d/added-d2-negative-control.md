Added a **negative-control soundness test** to the D2 pilot (`--negative-control`, ADR-093, #5684): for each export nanoda accepts, the driver also feeds it a deliberately **ill-typed copy** — two theorems' proof `value`s swapped, so a theorem's proof term no longer matches its claimed statement (the SPEC-049-A "claims X, proves Y" / weakened-statement class) — and **requires nanoda to reject it**. This tests the soundness property that actually matters: a checker that only *accepts valid* proofs fast is useless if it doesn't *reject invalid* ones. The report shows positive (target-theorem-present) and negative (ill-typed-rejected) controls; an *accept* of the ill-typed export is flagged a soundness failure. Observe-only; gates nothing.
