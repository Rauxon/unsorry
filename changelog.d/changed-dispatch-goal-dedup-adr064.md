The queue dispatcher (`agent.sh --dispatch-queue`) now opens **at most one prove PR per goal** ([ADR-064](docs/adrs/ADR-064-Goal-Level-Dispatch-Deduplication.md) / [SPEC-064-A](docs/adrs/specs/SPEC-064-A-Goal-Level-Dispatch-Deduplication.md)). The coordinated-submission model (ADR-058) let the prove-time claim race (knowingly deferred by ADR-017) accumulate duplicate `queued/prove/<goal>/*` branches — over half of a 653-branch queue covered only 316 distinct goals, and 26 goals were already proved on `main` — so draining it would have spent ~56% of namespace verifier capacity on proofs that can never merge (first-merge-wins) and only close as conflicts. The dispatcher now skips a queued branch whose goal is already proved on `main` (authoritative `library/index` marker, read from a freshly-fetched `origin/main`), already has an open prove PR, or was already handled earlier in the same pass — non-destructively, so a goal's sibling branches remain as fallbacks until one merges. This restores at the dispatch boundary the duplicate-work bound that ADR-017's PR-level hygiene provided before queue-mode moved duplicates off PRs and onto dormant branches. Recovers verifier capacity for *useful* proofs with no new runners; the upstream claim race and bulk pruning of existing redundant branches are tracked as follow-ups.
