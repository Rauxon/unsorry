The swarm no longer reports "nothing claimable" while dozens of open goals sit recoverable (ADR-044/SPEC-044-A). A prove goal that fails its first attempt is demoted below the viability floor (`affinity < TAU_V`), where `select_prove_candidates` can never surface it again — so once the viable goals are all in flight, `--prove` went idle even with many open goals parked (observed: 48 of 58 open goals orphaned at −10/−20, none re-attempted). When a `--prove` cycle finds no claimable viable goal, it now re-surfaces one goal parked below `TAU_V` (the new `recovery-candidates` selector, the exact inverse of `prove-candidates`, least-buried first) and runs it through the **same `prove_goal` pipeline** — so it retries with its accumulated `⟦Δ:Lesson⟧` history injected (ADR-024) and decomposes on failure (ADR-009), rather than starting from an empty board. One recovery goal per cycle, `HANDLED`/live-claim bounded, and disabled with `UNSORRY_RECOVERY=0`. The "no viable prove work" message now distinguishes in-flight/collided goals from the recovery attempt, and `--dry-run` reports the parked pool. New self-test `test_recovery_candidates`.
