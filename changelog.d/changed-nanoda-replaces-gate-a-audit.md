**Gate A throughput cutover (ADR-097 / SPEC-097-A):** the `gate_a_nanoda` job now replaces the 8-shard `axiom_audit` on the common leaf-proof path. It decides coverage cheaply (no build), and for a leaf proof runs a declaration-scoped `lean4export` + the pinned nanoda over the changed library closure with the axiom whitelist **hard-enforced** (a sneaked `sorryAx` fails) + the `pp_declars` positive control — in ~seconds on **one** runner instead of 8 audit shards. The real `axiom_audit` now runs **only** as the non-leaf / global-impact fallback (`covered != 'true'`), and the daily full-audit backstop is retained. **`leanchecker` replay stays `p = 1`** — this does NOT amend ADR-049's kernel oracle; nanoda's kernel check is additive diversity, the ADR-011 binding gate is retained. The aggregator is **fail-closed**: nanoda + replay are always required; the audit is required only on the fallback. Net: ~halves the per-PR runner footprint (drop the audit shards on leaf PRs) → lifts the Namespace-capacity-bound merge ceiling while `p=1` holds.
