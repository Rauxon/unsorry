Added a **trusted-side independent-check backstop** (`.github/workflows/independent-check-backstop.yml`, ADR-096 / SPEC-096-A §5b): a **scheduled, non-required, observe-only** workflow that re-checks recently-merged library proofs with an independent Lean kernel (nanoda) over a declaration-scoped lean4export — a second, kernel-diverse anchor that, unlike the contributor-side check, doesn't depend on a local prover winning a goal (it samples whatever lands on `main`, including the cloud swarm's proofs). It reuses the pilot driver (DRY) with the positive + negative soundness controls, and is **observe-only**: a disagreement (nanoda failing to accept a merged, leanchecker-verified proof) is surfaced as a `::warning::` + report, but the run succeeds — `nanoda` is pre-review (a beta-checker quirk must not red-alarm the schedule), and nothing is ever gated or reverted (ADR-049's p=1 Lean gate stays the sole oracle). Coexists with the contributor-side check; both paths are independent. Escalation to a failing run on a confirmed disagreement is deferred to after the SPEC-096-A §4 acceptance gates.
