Gate A's kernel replay now **parallelises by available RAM** instead of running strictly serially (ADR-047/SPEC-047-A). A FULL replay — forced by any gate/infra change (ADR-033) and routed to `namespace-profile-unsorry-2` — had been pinned to `--jobs 1` because two concurrent `leanchecker` processes (each holding ~all of mathlib, ~7 GB) once OOM-killed runners (#264). As the active library grew, that serial full replay crept past the 60-min timeout and was killed on every gate/infra PR, and **neither more CPU nor more RAM helped** (the work was `--jobs 1`). Replay now honours `--jobs "$(nproc)"`, capped by `max_safe_replay_jobs` at ~7 GB/process: it stays serial on a small runner (the condition that prevents the OOM) and fans out on a high-RAM one (e.g. ~8-way on 8 vCPU / 64 GB, turning a 60+ min serial replay into minutes). `gate-a-replay` timeout raised 60 → 120 as a safety net for the serial low-RAM path. Soundness is unchanged: parallel chunks produce identical per-module kernel verdicts; the only parallelism risk is OOM → a red gate (false negative), never a false pass, and the RAM cap bounds concurrency to what fits. **Operator step:** size `namespace-profile-unsorry-2` with RAM ≈ 7 GB × desired concurrency to realise the speed-up.
